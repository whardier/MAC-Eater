Because you can't block the signal.

Your laptops, desktops, servers, webcams, personal digital assistants (PDA), phones, switchs, routers, wireless access points, and bluetooth devices have a set of numbers associated with them by the manufacturer called a (MAC) address. Every time a device is sent a packet on an IP network ARP is used to decice what IP goes to what computer based on the MAC address. This means a device routing your packets or even just near you knows the MAC address of your device. This is where we come in.
